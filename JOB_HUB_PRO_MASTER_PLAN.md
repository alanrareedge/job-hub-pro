\# Job Hub Pro - Master Project Context



\## Vision



Job Hub Pro is the operating system for modern trades businesses.



Its purpose is to help trades businesses:



\- Win better work.

\- Price work properly.

\- Present proposals professionally.

\- Deliver jobs efficiently.

\- Build a more valuable business with every customer, every property and every job.



Job Hub Pro is not simply a CRM or job management system.



It combines operational workflow, commercial intelligence and business coaching.



GoHighLevel provides the communication layer.



Job Hub Pro provides the operational intelligence layer.

\## Product Principles



\- The software should guide, not just record.

\- The software should reflect how tradespeople think, not how databases store information.

\- Price the outcome, not just the hours.

\- Pricing and Proposal are different disciplines.

\- Simplicity beats feature count.

\- Build intelligence, not duplication.

\- Every module should earn its place.

\- The software should coach quietly.

\- Protect margin at every opportunity.

\- The system should help prevent margin leakage.

\- Materials and suppliers are strategic assets.

\- Trade knowledge belongs to the business.

\- Every module should be capable of becoming exceptional in its own right.

\- Every action should build a business asset.

\## Pricing Engine Philosophy



The Pricing Engine answers:



"What should we charge?"



It should:



\- Protect profit.

\- Make assumptions explicit.

\- Make exclusions explicit.

\- Recommend pricing.

\- Build confidence.



It should not:



\- Generate proposals.

\- Produce PDFs.

\- Handle acceptance.



\---



\## Proposal Builder Philosophy



The Proposal Builder answers:



"How should we present it?"



It should:



\- Reuse pricing data.

\- Embed Million Dollar Words principles.

\- Generate confidence.

\- Support one, two or three-option proposals.

\- Allow businesses to customise option labels.



Examples:



\- Good / Better / Best

\- Essential / Recommended / Premium

\- Bronze / Silver / Gold

\- Basic / Professional / Ultimate



Internally these remain:



\- Option 1

\- Option 2

\- Option 3



The psychology stays consistent while the wording remains flexible.

\## Development Rules



\- Build in small phases.

\- Mobile first.

\- Security by default.

\- Soft delete using archived\_at.

\- One feature at a time.

\- Do not over-engineer.

\- Always preserve the long-term architecture.

\- Never build future modules early.
